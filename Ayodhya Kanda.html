Ayodhya Kanda

The Ayodhya Kanda is a pivotal section of the ancient Indian epic, the Ramayana. In this Kanda, the story primarily revolves around the events leading to Lord Rama's exile from Ayodhya. The key highlights of the Ayodhya Kanda include the following: The narrative begins with the idyllic city of Ayodhya, ruled by King Dasharatha, who has three queens: Kaushalya, Kaikeyi, and Sumitra. Dasharatha wishes to crown his eldest son, Rama, as his successor, much to the joy of the kingdom. However, Kaikeyi's scheming maid Manthara influences her to demand that her own son, Bharata, be crowned instead, and Rama be exiled to the forest for fourteen years. Despite Dasharatha's pleas, Rama accepts the exile decree as his duty and vows to fulfill his father's word. His devoted wife, Sita, and loyal brother, Lakshmana, accompany him willingly. The people of Ayodhya are grief-stricken by Rama's departure, and the city descends into mourning. In the midst of sorrow, Bharata returns from a visit to his maternal grandparents and learns of the events that have transpired. Horrified by his mother's actions, Bharata refuses to accept the throne and sets out to bring Rama back to Ayodhya. Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana venture deep into the forests, encountering various sages, demons, and celestial beings during their exile. Their unwavering adherence to dharma and righteousness earns them respect and admiration along their journey. Meanwhile, back in Ayodhya, Bharata attempts to persuade Rama to return and rule as the rightful king. He places Rama's sandals on the throne as a symbol of his authority and governs the kingdom in Rama's absence. The Ayodhya Kanda concludes with Bharata's continued efforts to convince Rama to return, while Rama remains steadfast in his commitment to fulfill his father's promise. The stage is set for the subsequent Kandas, where the epic tale of the Ramayana unfolds further, leading to the eventual confrontation with the demon king Ravana.
