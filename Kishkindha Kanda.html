Kishkindha Kanda

The Kishkindha Kanda is a significant section of the ancient Indian epic Ramayana. In this part of the narrative, Lord Rama, accompanied by his loyal brother Lakshmana, continues his search for his abducted wife Sita. Their quest leads them to the kingdom of Kishkindha, where they encounter Sugriva, the exiled vanara (monkey) king, and his trusted minister, Hanuman. Sugriva had been ousted from his kingdom by his own brother Vali, who unjustly accused him of treachery. Upon learning of Rama's mission and prowess, Sugriva seeks an alliance with him. In return, Rama requests Sugriva's assistance in locating Sita. To solidify their pact, Rama helps Sugriva defeat Vali in combat, thereby restoring Sugriva's kingship. Subsequently, Sugriva dispatches search parties in all directions to locate Sita, with Hanuman leading the most significant effort. During this time, Hanuman discovers Sita's whereabouts in Lanka, the island kingdom ruled by the demon king Ravana. Hanuman conveys this vital information to Rama, strengthening their resolve to rescue Sita and ultimately confront Ravana. The Kishkindha Kanda sets the stage for Hanuman's legendary journey to Lanka, which unfolds in the subsequent section of the epic.
