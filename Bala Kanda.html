Bala Kanda

The Bala Kanda, the opening section of the ancient Indian epic, the Ramayana, lays the groundwork for the epic's narrative. It begins with King Dasharatha of Ayodhya performing a grand sacrifice to have children, which results in the birth of four sons, the eldest being Rama. Rama grows up to be a noble and righteous prince, known for his exemplary character even from his childhood days. One of the pivotal events in the Bala Kanda is Rama's marriage to Sita, the daughter of King Janaka of Mithila. Rama wins Sita's hand by accomplishing the seemingly impossible task of stringing the divine bow of Lord Shiva. This event not only strengthens the bond between Rama and Sita but also initiates their epic love story. However, the joyous occasion is marred by the manipulative schemes of Kaikeyi, one of King Dasharatha's wives. Under the influence of her maid Manthara, Kaikeyi demands that her son Bharata be made the heir to the throne instead of Rama. Reluctantly, Dasharatha agrees and sends Rama into exile in the forest for fourteen years. Accompanied by his devoted wife Sita and loyal brother Lakshmana, Rama embarks on his journey into the wilderness. Thus, the Bala Kanda serves as an introduction to the characters and central conflicts of the Ramayana, setting the stage for the epic's subsequent events.
